  • Evaluation of Teaching Effectiveness

    In the last three years, IU students have frequently rated me above the 90th percentile among all humanities courses for rapport and respect, fairness, enthusiasm, preparedness, knowledge of course topics, and intellectual stimulation.

    Ninety-four percent of my Summer 2010 F250 class would recommend me to other students, and one of my Fall 2012 F150 sections rated me as an overall “outstanding” instructor (96.7th percentile). In prior years, students at Penn State Altoona appreciated my friendliness, preparation, and the way I encouraged them to think and participate, and feel free to ask questions. (Sample evaluations available upon request.)

    Read more...

    Mid-semester and end-of-semester evaluations have helped clarify where students feel they are succeeding and where I need to improve my approach. Following are some examples of what they have written about my teaching:

    • “Very willing to help outside of class”
    • “Erin was always very enthusiastic in class, and made it lively and fun! She’s also a very good teacher and is able to explain grammar points very well.

    • “Erin is extremely professional. She always speaks French and does an exceptional job explaining the meaning of a word in French. […] She personally sees to make sure everyone understands.”
    • "Enthusiasm, ability to draw students into the language, compassion, recognition of where students struggle.”
