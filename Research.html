Research

My dissertation research focuses on the life and works of Jean-Baptiste Pierre Antoine de Monet, chevalier de Lamarck, and particularly the ways in which his style and thought constitute a pivotal moment in French natural philosophy writing, and inspiration for a generation of nineteenth-century novelists. I will defend my dissertation at IU in May 2015. My doctoral minor in History and Philosophy of Science has provided me with some of the tools and knowledge which are key to that discipline. In the future, I would like to continue to conduct research which makes connections between the philosophy and science of the early modern period and 19th, 20th, and 21st-century adaptations of these ideas. Please contact me if you would like a copy of my research statement or dissertation abstract.

My other interests are broad. I am fascinated by the early modern period in France and the long Enlightenment. In my F300 course in Spring 2014, I was able to combine my interest in science in the early modern period with an inquiry into the contributions that women and femininity made to the creation of knowledge in this period. I have presented research on Choderlos de Laclos’s Les liaisons dangereuses and Gustave Flaubert’s L’Education sentimentale. Courses that I have taken, and courses that I have developed and taught have permitted me to develop a working knowledge of many classic and lesser-known French authors, poets, and film producers, including Marie de France, Chrétien de Troyes, Du Bellay, Pascal, Madeleine de Scudéry, Rousseau, Diderot, Emilie du Châtelet, Sainte-Beuve, Balzac, Victor Hugo, George Sand, Flaubert, Rachilde, Jean Renoir and Yves Bonnefoy. I read many other authors, periods, and literatures for fun in Old French, Italian, and German, as well as in French and English. Some genres that I particularly enjoy include poetry, novels, science, and literature of the fantastique.
