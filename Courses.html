    I was pleased to be able to offer this bridge course to the French Major on a topic of my choosing, and to have the opportunity to work with the Center for Innovative Teaching and Learning (CITL) at IU to include novel and interesting course assignments and technologies.

  • Not Quite Human (Indiana University, Fall 2013)

    This course represents my first opportunity to teach an undergraduate literature course at IU. I chose the theme, readings, and arranged IU Art Museum visits to enrich this survey course which included readings from the Middle Ages to the 20th century and diverse genres. Class discussions and activities worked to build vocabulary, and three writing assignments gave students the chance to hone their grammar and expression. As a class, we planned and hosted a Revolutionary festival, the Sansculotides.

    Related document:

  • Literature Survey Course (IU Honors Program in Foreign Languages, Saumur, Summer 2011)

    As the Literature Instructor for the summer program in Saumur, France, I designed a literature survey course including authors from the Pays de Loire, and fiction and non-fiction genres from many periods of French literature in order to explore the relationships between history, literature, and the land.

  • Future Courses to Develop

    Following is a sample list of literature courses which I would be interested in developing and teaching in French or in English. I imagine adapting them according to the curricular needs of the department or college. They represent interests of mine which I have developed through graduate coursework and research. The topics are quite diverse, but for each idea there is a corresponding nucleus of texts which would allow for both breadth and depth in the course approach. Depending on the topic, various forms of evaluation of learning would be appropriate, from traditional forms (exam, essay, analyse de texte) to non-traditional forms (blog, Wikipedia entry, artistic books, performances, oral examination, film review, scale models, conference participation, etc.).
