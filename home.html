About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in French Literature at Indiana University, and have taught as a Visiting Lecturer and F150 Course Supervisor in the Department of French and Italian at IU from 2012-2014. I have ten years of teaching experience (French and English) and have taught French language, literature, and culture in a variety of contexts. My French “home” area is the north: I lived for one year in Amiens and one year in Lille. I enjoy traveling and the outdoors, and have explored many different regions of France, as well as Belgium, Germany, Switzerland, Hungary, Ireland, Quebec, and Honduras. I don’t have a hometown in the USA, but have lived in many different states, including Massachusetts, Minnesota, Pennsylvania, and Indiana. Most summers I spend a little time in northern Minnesota fishing, canoeing, swimming, and relaxing with my family. Thank you for your interest in my teaching, research, and professional activity.
